Experience

I first learned to code up my own website in 2004 when I was teaching and wanted a non-university-branded place on the internet to call my own. I didn't get to focus further on this endeavor until 10 years later, at a small art non-profit, where some of my duties were to upkeep and develop three websites, one legacy and two WordPress.

Since early 2017, I have increased my familiarity with CSS and HTML as I've maintained and built upon the website of Ke Ola Magazine, and have exercised my graphic skills there as well, becoming quite skilled in Photoshop and InDesign.

My lifelong love of logic — and all of the persnickety detail-orientation it requires — makes coding naturally attractive for me, much more so than working with template website builders like WordPress, although understanding coding certainly makes it easier to navigate their structure. I look forward to learning even more about back-end coding and be of better service to clients needing website assistance.
